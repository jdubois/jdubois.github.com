## Anti-Patterns Verdict
Does not look AI-generated. Detector scan of index.html = clean (0 findings). Distinctive grotesk + mono identity reads as deliberate. The problem is composition, not slop.

## Overall Impression
The brand identity is strong and professional. But the page wastes a large amount of space: a tall portrait pins the hero, leaving (a) a ~300px empty band ABOVE the name and (b) the entire right half blank below the hero, where the bio text is confined to a narrow left column. The result reads "half-finished" rather than "confident minimalism."

## Priority Issues

### [P1] The right half of the page is empty below the hero
The intro bio (3 paragraphs + list) sits in a full-width section but `p { max-width: 68ch }` confines it to the left ~55%. The right ~45% is blank from the stat strip to the footer. This is the single biggest source of "empty space."
**Fix:** Bring the portrait DOWN beside the bio (two-column body), or give the lower section real content in the right column (a "Featured projects" / "Latest" panel, talk highlights, or a pull-quote), or center the prose as a single measured column so there is no lopsided void.

### [P1] Vertical void in the hero left column
With the tagline removed, the left column (name + mono line + buttons) is short, but `.home-hero .row { align-items: center }` vertically centers it against the tall portrait — producing a big empty band above the name and below the buttons.
**Fix:** `align-items: start` and reduce portrait height (cap it, e.g. `max-height` or a smaller column), or add the stat strip / a short intro line back into the left column so both columns balance.

### [P2] Portrait is oversized and dominates
At 1440px the portrait is ~620px tall and visually outweighs the name. It's a strong photo, but it's doing too much of the talking.
**Fix:** Constrain it (smaller column or capped height with `object-fit: cover`), freeing vertical space and rebalancing toward the name/identity.

### [P2] Page is short and bottom-heavy on whitespace
After the bio list, the page ends abruptly into the footer with no closing element, reinforcing the empty feeling on large screens.
**Fix:** Add a compact closing band (e.g. "Currently / Projects / Talks" links or a CTA row) so the page resolves instead of trailing off.

## Persona Red Flags
**Recruiter / conference organizer (first-time visitor):** Lands on a striking name + photo but, on a wide monitor, sees a lot of blank canvas; may read the page as sparse or under-built rather than intentional. The credibility stats are good but get lost in the void.
**Developer peer:** Wants to jump to projects/code; the empty right half is a missed opportunity to surface JHipster / LangChain4j / talks directly.

## Minor Observations
- The stat strip and bio share the same left edge but the right side has nothing to anchor against — a right-aligned element would tie the composition together.
- Footer is minimal; fine, but adds to the "ends in emptiness" feel.

## Questions to Consider
- What if the portrait sat beside the bio (filling the right column) instead of towering over the hero alone?
- What would fill the empty right half with something useful — featured projects, latest talk, a short "currently" note?
- Does the hero need a tall portrait, or a tighter, top-aligned composition?
