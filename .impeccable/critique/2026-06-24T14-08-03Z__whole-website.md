## Anti-Patterns Verdict
- Deterministic scan: detect.mjs across all pages returned `[]` (no hard tells: no gradient text, no eyebrows, no fake metrics, no side-stripes in content).
- LLM assessment: reads as the "Bootstrap + Inter personal site" monoculture — white bg, Inter (on the reflex-reject list), single blue accent, rounded cards with hover-lift, uniform fade-in-up on load. Not egregious slop, but fails the brand-register distinctiveness bar: "safe = invisible."

## What's Working
1. Solid IA and clean, consistent shell (nav, footer, spacing) — easy to navigate.
2. Good real photography (portrait, Devoxx, Microsoft) — the raw assets are strong.
3. Conferences page (recently polished) is the best page: collapsibles, surfaced CTAs, clear sections.

## Priority Issues
- [P1] No distinctive visual identity. White + Inter + one blue + Bootstrap cards is indistinguishable from thousands of dev sites; for the creator of JHipster / Java Champion / Microsoft, the site undersells the person. Fix: a deliberate type pairing (drop Inter), a signature color/accent system, stronger hero. Highest leverage for "professional & good-looking."
- [P1] Home & Bio have no <h1>; Home leads with a generic "Hello!". Wastes the most valuable space and hurts SEO/outline. Fix: real hero — name as h1 + one-line positioning.
- [P2] Copy bugs on Book page: "édtion"→"édition", "rutpure"→"rupture". Contact email is a literal placeholder `hello@[the current domain name]` — give a real copyable/clickable address.
- [P2] Inconsistent components: social-card icons mix crisp SVG (X) with low-res PNGs (GitHub, LinkedIn); Photos page is an unstructured stack of differently-sized images (no grid/captions); project pages are walls of gray text with no logo/screenshot.
- [P2] Motion has no reduced-motion guard: fadeIn/fadeInUp on hero/content/cards lack a prefers-reduced-motion alternative (only .talk has one); .content-section starts at opacity:0 via `both`.
- [P3] Body text uniformly muted gray (#6b7280) reads low-energy over long blocks; short pages leave large empty lower halves.

## Persona Red Flags
- Conference organizer / hiring manager (5-second test on Home): sees "Hello!", not "who is this and why he matters"; the JHipster/Java-Champion punch is buried in paragraph 3.
- Journalist wanting to email: Contact email is a placeholder string to decode — friction at the key conversion point.

## Minor Observations
- Same portrait reused on Home and Bio.
- `.dropdown-item.active` / mobile `.nav-link.active` use a 3px border-left (side-stripe) — pre-existing, low priority.
- Inter loaded via @import (render-blocking); minor perf.
